I am a Doctoral candidate in the [School of Criminology and Criminal Justice](https://www.unomaha.edu/college-of-public-affairs-and-community-service/criminology-and-criminal-justice/index.php](https://www.unomaha.edu/college-of-public-affairs-and-community-service/criminology-and-criminal-justice/about-us/sarah-al-falatah.php) at the University of Nebraska at Omaha. I am a health criminologist, with an emphasis on mental and physical health within the criminal justice system and a secondary focus on violence prevention and program evaluation. My research explores health conditions as both precursors and consequences of criminal justice involvement and victimization. Specifically, I examine how mental and physical health shape pathways into offending and victimization, and how those experiences may amplify future health conditions. My dissertation evaluates the implementation and effectiveness of a Hospital-Based Violence Intervention Program (HVIP) (view [my CV](files/AlFalatah_CV_04_26.pdf)).
